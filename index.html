Foot Tightness & Mobility Library

For arch tightness or cramping, or right after planks and hikes.
1. Kettlebell / Tennis Ball Arch Rollout
Stand near a wall. Place a tennis ball or kettlebell handle flat on the floor. Step on it with a bare foot, moderate pressure, and roll from heel to ball of foot. Hold hot spots 20–30 sec.
1–2 minutes per foot
2. Seated Plantar Fascia Stretch
Cross your ankle over the opposite knee. Pull your toes back toward your shin with one hand, and gently massage the tight arch tissue with the other.
Hold 30 sec, repeat 2–3x per side
3. Toe Curls & Splay (Intrinsic Strength)
Feet flat on the floor. Lift only the big toe, keeping the other four flat. Swap it: big toe down, lift the four small toes. Then splay all toes as wide as possible.
60 seconds total
4. The "Toe Sit" Hero Pose
Kneel with knees together, tuck your toes completely under to sit on the balls of your feet, and slowly sit your hips back onto your heels. Lean forward onto your hands to reduce the load if it's too intense.
Hold 30–60 seconds
